## Well-covered issues

- Independently fitted human and LLM latent scales are not linked by a common
  metric or measurement-invariance argument.
- The paper interprets Phase-2 results for conditions that fail the stated
  Phase-1 gate, especially VIEScore.
- The CV definition needs a justification for `|K_p|-1`, explicit handling of
  empty/singleton categories, and sensitivity analysis.
- The fixed CV/rho thresholds are heuristics, not validated universal cutoffs.
- GRM assumptions, human annotation aggregation, bootstrap units, multiple
  testing, dataset statistics, perturbation validity, and vision generalization
  need stronger reporting or validation.

## Material omissions

1. Equation (7)'s Wasserstein distance compares only marginal theta
   distributions and discards sample pairing. Permuting LLM theta values among
   samples leaves the distance unchanged, so it cannot by itself measure human
   correspondence. Table 8's VIEScore Pearson correlations of approximately
   -0.25 to 0.22 make this limitation concrete.
2. The Appendix prompt templates contain rating-scale conflicts. SummEval says
   1-3 but requests 1-5 JSON; TopicalChat detail and detail+CoT also change the
   scale, confounding the claimed CoT ablation.
3. Appendix B says `json` is protected from typo perturbation, while Table 4
   reports `JSON -> SJON`.
4. Table 10 contains multiple point-estimate swaps relative to Table 1,
   including VIEScore CIG-SC/PQ rows and several TopicalChat model columns.
5. Several narrative ranges disagree with the tables, including SummEval rho,
   Gemini NLP CV, NLP theta ratio, VIEScore PQ, and Pearson correlation ranges.

These are not minor presentation issues. The first directly challenges the
central Phase-2 validity claim; the prompt and table inconsistencies weaken the
reported ablations and result reliability. An independent paper check therefore
supports Overall Recommendation 2 (Reject), or at minimum a stronger rationale
for the pipeline's score 3.

## Pipeline-specific caution

`author_rebuttal.md` is generated by the pipeline from the paper and initial
review. It is not an actual response from the paper authors. Consequently, the
final review's references to "the author response" are simulation output and
must not be presented as an observed author commitment.
